    • Daily COVID-19 Cases Analysis in 2021

    Malaysia reported a total of 2,147,675 COVID-19 Cases in 2021 with several infection waves. In this year, cases have reached all time highs multiple times, with the highest number of cases being 24,599 on the 26th August. Ever since February, Malaysia has been ramping up its vaccination progress, and has undergone lockdown for a total of three times.

    Where are these cases coming from?

    Despite having several total lockdowns in Malaysia, our cases does not seem to fall. This might be explained by the fact that work places such as factories are still allowed to operate, even during the lockdown period. Without surprise, majority of the cases fall within that category.

    What is the vaccination progress in Malaysia?

    Ever since 24th of February, the Malaysian Government has started its mass vaccination program. As of 30th of September, we can see that more than half than the population of Malaysia has been vaccinated. We are projected to reach 80% nationwide immunity in 26th of November if the vaccine momentum is maintained.

    Where is Malaysia headed?

    9th October 2021- In a national address, Malaysian Prime Minister Ismail Sabri Yaakob said that the state is headed for a major re-opening after hitting an important vaccination milestone. Relaxation of interstate and overseas travel will start effectively on the 11th of October for individuals who are adults and fully vaccinated against COVID-19. However, those who enter Malaysia will need need to undergo a 14-day quarantine at government provided locations even if vaccinated. The PM warned that Malaysians will have to comply with COVID-19 Standard Operating Procedures, and will be fined if caught violated.
